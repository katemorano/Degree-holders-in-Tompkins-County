Over half the population of this rural county in New York have bachelor's degrees

Tompkins County in the Southern Tier region of the state defies the status quo, with nearly 60% of it's residents holding a bachelor's degree or higher despite the majority of the county being farmland

By Kate Morano | December 20, 2024

Generally, educated Americans congregate in large metropolitan areas and New York is mostly consistent with this trend, but Tompkins County is an anomaly. Less than 50 miles from the New York/Pennsylvania border, it is one of the smallest counties in the state at 476 square feet. Around a third of this area is farmland. And yet, it is the second most educated county in New York, outpaced only by Manhattan.

One of the most prestigious universities in the country is the cause of this phenomenon. Cornell University, a member of the Ivy League, is located in the city of Ithaca, Tompkins County’s seat. Ithaca, while not an exceptionally large city, is a city on the rise; it continues to add jobs and grow it’s population while other cities in Upstate New York lag behind. About 1 in 5 workers in the county are employed by Cornell University, according to the college’s website, and around 30% of the county’s population are full-time college students.
